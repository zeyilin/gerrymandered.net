About Broadbandti.me

Broadbandti.me was created by Yash Aggarwal, Sami Glasco, and Zeyi Lin during the weekend of January 15 - 17, 2016, for the 2016 HackRice hackathon.

The website scrapes the websites of various Internet Service Providers (ISPs) in the United States to provide the user with the best value (transfer speed per unit price). Broadbandti.me also allows users to compare the service speeds from providers near them against publicly available government broadband data.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is broadband?

Broadband internet is defined by the United States Federal Communications Commission to be Internet service speeds of 25 megabits per second (Mbps) for downloads and 3 Mbps for uploads.1

What do these numbers mean?

A megabit is equal to 106 bits, 1,000 kilobits (103 bits), or 1,000,000 bits. A megabit per second (Mbps) is a measure of the data transfer rate, or the amount of data transferred per unit time.2 Using the FCC definition above, a data rate of 25 Mbps means that 25,000,000 bits are transferred per second.

Why do my download and upload speeds matter?

Higher download speeds means that you'll be able to receive information on your computer or device from the web. Download speeds are important for web browsing, streaming multimedia, and downloading larger files online. Upload speed, on the other hand, is "the speed at which you can send information... to the web."3 Uses of a more robust upload speed include video conferencing, sharing files online, interactive learning, telemedicine, gaming, and cloud computing.

Broadband as a platform enables a range of web applications, ranging from products, services, processes, and business models,4 such as streaming multimedia (videos, movies, TV shows, and music), improve the Internet experience for multiple users connected to a network, video conferencing, significantly improved download and upload speeds, and better performance in online gaming.5

The table below provides suggested use cases at various Internet service speeds:

Speed Use Case
1-2 Mbps Light web use (basic email, web surfing, video streaming, video conferencing, or online gaming) for a small number of users on a network
6-15 Mbps Moderate web use for a sizable number of users on a network (basic functions and streaming music or high definition video content, e.g. Spotify, Apple Music, Netflix)
More than 15 Mbps Multiple high demand web applications for multiple users at the same time (e.g. multiple HD web streams or web video conferences)
25 Mbps Recommended for Ultra High Definition (HD) Netflix video streaming

How can I test my current Internet service speeds?

Speedtest.net offers a widely-used tool that allows you to test your Internet service speeds right from your web browser. Speedtest.net also offers an iOS app, Android app, and Windows Phone app for mobile users.

The FCC also offers an iOS app and an Android app that also allow users to test their mobile Internet speeds or Internet speeds while connected to a Wi-Fi network.
